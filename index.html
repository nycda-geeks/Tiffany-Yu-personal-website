Diversability

I am the founder of Diversability, a social movement to rebrand disability through the power of community. We started as a campus organization at Georgetown University in 2009 and relaunched to the broader New York community in 2015. I became passionate about disability inclusion and empowerment after a car accident when I was younger left me with a severe nerve injury in one of my arms.

Since our relaunch, our grassroots efforts have grown to Los Angeles, Montgomery, Alabama, and San Francisco. Online, we built our community from the ground up to almost 6,000 followers reaching over 600,000 monthly.

We have been featured in the Guardian, Psychology Today, and others. We were excited to be a part of award-winning photographer Rick Guidotti's Positive Exposure Pop-Up Center in New York City in 2016. We were also honored to receive the 2015 Bell Greve Memorial Award from the National Rehabilitation Association.
